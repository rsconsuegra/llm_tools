# Plot Quality Check

Evaluate the plot outline against these rules. Report violations (must fix), warnings (should fix), and observations (worth noting).

## Protagonist Quality

- **Has a meaningful lie**: The protagonist believes something false about themselves or the world that drives poor decisions. "Doesn't believe in herself" is a warning (too generic). "Believes mercy is weakness because her father was killed for showing it" passes.
- **Has a ghost**: A past event that haunts them and shapes their present behavior. Must be specific — not "a tragic past" but a named event with consequences.
- **Has internal contradiction**: Their want and need must pull in opposite directions. If the protagonist can get what they want and grow as a person simultaneously, the tension is insufficient.
- **Has strong opinions or biases**: The protagonist should have specific, debatable viewpoints. Neutral protagonists who merely react are a warning.
- **Faces pressure against comfort zone**: The plot must force the protagonist into situations they would normally avoid.

Violation if the protagonist has none of these. Warning if they have only 1-2.

## Beat Quality

- **Causality**: Each beat must cause the next. If beats can be reordered without changing the story, flag as a warning. If beats have no connecting logic, flag as a violation.
- **Escalation**: Stakes and tension must increase across acts. Three consecutive beats at similar intensity is a warning.
- **Midpoint shift**: The midpoint must change the direction of the story (false victory, false defeat, or major revelation). A midpoint that continues the rising action pattern is a violation.
- **No redundant beats**: Two beats that serve the same narrative function should be flagged as a warning.

## Premise Quality

- **Built-in tension**: The core conflict must be present from the premise, not introduced later. "A girl goes on an adventure" is a violation. "A girl must betray her sister to save her village" passes.
- **Central contradiction**: The premise should contain an irresolvable tension that drives the story. Two good things that cannot coexist, or two bad outcomes where one must be chosen.
- **Real pressure**: The protagonist must face consequences for inaction. If the protagonist could simply choose not to act and nothing bad happens, flag as a violation.
- **Audience hook**: The premise should raise a specific question the audience wants answered. "Will she succeed?" is not a hook. "Will she sacrifice her voice to save her brother?" passes.

## Structure Conformance (if a structure model is referenced)

- Beat positions should roughly align with the referenced structure's expected percentages (within 10%).
- All required beats for the referenced structure should be present.
- Flag missing beats as warnings, not violations — creative deviation from structure is valid.
